BBM92 Protocol Simulation

The BBM92 protocol (Bennett-Brassard-Mermin 1992) is an entanglement-based Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) protocol. Instead of preparing and sending individual quantum states (as in BB84), a central source emits pairs of entangled photons distributed to Alice and Bob. They independently measure the photons in randomly chosen bases. Due to quantum correlations, their measurements yield identical bit sequences, from which a secure cryptographic key can be established. This simulator estimates both the Secret Key Rate (SKR) and Quantum Bit Error Rate (QBER) over varying distances and channels.
